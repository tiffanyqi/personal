About

Welcome! I'm Tiffany Qi, and I am a third year Business Administration major studying Business, Computer Science, and Applied Linguistics at the University of California Berkeley. I am pursuing a career in product management as well as in human resources and recruiting.

I have discovered over the past couple of years through my many involvements on campus that I am passionate in two things: helping others and making an impact. In total, I have helped approximately 200+ students, and this number only grows by the day! I strive to make life on Earth a little easier for everyone, and it's a statement that I live by strongly. I have struggled just like everyone else, but I want to make sure there is someone there to help when something happens. I specalize in management, logistics, and planning; and as such, I take care of organizational logistics so that others can focus on what they love.

I am currently President and Co-Founder of new student organization Computer Science Mentors and previous Vice President of Marketing Community at Berkeley, two Berkeley organizations that are focused in providing students with resources and tutoring opportunities to succeed at Cal and upon graduation. In addition, I spun up a project called ursaMajor, which (upon completion) will provide students with the ability to plan out their four years while noting their graduation requirements! In these projects and organizations, I am focused on creating a strong base for what members do best: program, design, teach, and learn.

I am also an advisor and blogger with CalAsk and Advice from a Haas Major, one that provides students' with answers to all their questions, and the other is a personal project I've created that guides pre-Haas students for admission. The blog has reached 9,000 views since its creation! I am dedicated to ensuring this campus is as supported as possible, and I won't stop until every student has their questions answered.

Feel free to get in contact with me through email. Scroll to learn a bit more about me!
